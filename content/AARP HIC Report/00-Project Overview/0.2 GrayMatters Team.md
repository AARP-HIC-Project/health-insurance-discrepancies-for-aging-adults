# GrayMatters Marketing
## About
[GrayMatters Marketing, LLC](https://www.linkedin.com/company/graymatters-marketing-llc/) is a [[Qualitative Research|qualitative research]] and marketing firm that focuses on eliciting and translating target customer insights which inform the development and execution of programs that drive brand image, preference and sales.

 The GrayMatters service platform provides clients a unique combination of “in the trenches” marketing strategy/planning expertise, consultative approach and moderating skills that deliver insights and recommendations which fuel business growth. Our management team consists of classically trained marketing strategists with experience in Beverages, Consumer and Luxury Goods, Communications, Fashion, Financial Services, Pharmaceuticals, QSR and Retail categories.
 
GrayMatters Marketing focuses its qualitative and strategic services in four core areas:
• Brand Strategy
• Business Strategy
• Target Insights and
• Marketing Program Development/Evaluation

## Strategic Research Services
Recently, GrayMatters has expanded its offerings to include [[Strategic Research Services]] focus on a comprehensive review of the current state of affairs pertaining to a client's specific topic of interest. Through rigorous analysis, GrayMatters delivers actionable recommendations derived from robust data, empowering organizations with critical foresight.

This allows clients to navigate the complex challenges of today, while building their programs and brands with the foresight of the future. This strategic expansion underscores GrayMatters' commitment to equipping its clients with the necessary tools to make informed decisions and foster sustainable growth in an evolving landscape.

## Darius Bittle-Dockery
![[Pasted image 20250625132949.png|300]]

Darius is an accomplished applied social science researcher with over a decade of expert training and hands-on experience. His expertise lies in conducting ethnographic research and executing end-to-end research projects with a variety of businesses, non-profits, and international NGOs. He has successfully partnered with a wide variety of organizations, including Fortune 500 businesses, impactful non-profits, and leading international NGOs across sectors like healthcare, technology, and community development.

Darius has worked on interdisciplinary research projects ranging from improving the information architecture of humanitarian health organizations by enhancing information delivery in crisis-affected regions, to providing financial literacy and professional mentorship for disenfranchised BIPOC youth that empowers them with essential tools for economic mobility and personal growth.

In these multifaceted and often challenging settings, Darius consistently demonstrates an exceptional ability to translate complex qualitative and quantitative data into actionable insights and strategic recommendations, effectively turning raw information into powerful drivers for sustainable positive change. His academic foundation includes a PhD in Medical Anthropology and a MPH in Behavioral and Community Health Sciences from the University of Pittsburgh, as well as a BA in Anthropology from Tufts University.

## Rebecca Martinez
![[Pasted image 20250625131643.png|300]]

Rebecca is a social science researcher and applied scholar with nearly a decade of experience in [[Qualitative Research]], specializing in health equity and its intersections with gender and education. She brings deep expertise in using ethnographic methods to inform the design of systems, services, and policies that advance more inclusive and effective health outcomes. Her work includes significant contributions to global health initiatives aimed at improving health outcomes, as well as community-based projects examining the impact of COVID-19 on Midwestern communities.

Rebecca is currently a PhD candidate at Purdue University, where she uses ethnographic methods to study how institutional structures, workplace cultures, and power dynamics affect collaboration, decision-making, and the implementation of health-related interventions. Her research offers practical insight into how organizations can better support equity-focused initiatives in healthcare and education. This specialization, combined with her academic background in International Relations and Business Administration, strengthens her ability to translate complex research insights into practical, impactful solutions.

## Graylin Skates 
![[Pasted image 20250625125919.png|300]]

Graylin holds an MS from the Department of Anthropology at Purdue University, where she focused on health services research and Medical Anthropology. Her thesis evaluated public health programs that navigate patients through disjointed health systems and considered the impact of health policy and insurance systems on the provision of care.

Graylin has contributed to interdisciplinary research projects that assess patient experience with family planning services, vascular surgery, and cervical cancer screening. In her applied work, Graylin has managed the patient care coordination and administrative operations for a non-profit clinic serving under/uninsured people in North Florida, consulted on reproductive health promotion activities for people experiencing homelessness, and fostered community partnerships for non-profit pediatric health organizations. Graylin also holds a BA in Anthropology and BS in Microbiology from the University of Florida.
