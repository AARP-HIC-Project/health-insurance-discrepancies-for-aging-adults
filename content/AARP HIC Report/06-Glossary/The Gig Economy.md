**Term:**  
`The Gig Economy`

**Definition:** 
The gig economy is a labor market characterized by the prevalence of short-term contracts or freelance work, as opposed to permanent jobs. Individuals, often referred to as "gig workers," perform tasks or provide services on a flexible, on-demand basis, typically facilitated by digital platforms that connect them with clients or customers. This economic model emphasizes temporary, project-based work, allowing for greater flexibility for workers and lower overhead for businesses.

**Related Terms:**  
- **Synonyms:** Freelance economy, platform economy, on-demand economy, contingent workforce  
- **Antonyms:** Traditional employment, full-time employment, permanent employment
