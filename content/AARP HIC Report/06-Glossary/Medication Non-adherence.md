**Term:**
`medication non-adherence`

**Definition:**
Medication non-adherence refers to the extent to which a patient's behavior concerning taking medication, following dietary restrictions, or executing lifestyle changes deviates from the recommendations provided by a healthcare professional. This broad term encompasses various forms of deviation, including not filling prescriptions, taking incorrect dosages, missing doses, stopping medication prematurely, or taking medication at the wrong time. Such deviations from prescribed treatment regimens can significantly impact health outcomes, treatment efficacy, and overall disease management.

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** medication non-compliance, poor adherence, suboptimal adherence
