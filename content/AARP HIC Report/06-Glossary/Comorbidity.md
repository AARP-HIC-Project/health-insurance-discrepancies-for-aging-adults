**Term:**
`comorbidity`

**Definition:**
Comorbidity refers to the simultaneous presence of two or more medical conditions or diseases in a patient. These conditions are often chronic and may interact with each other, influencing the course, prognosis, and treatment of each individual disease. The concept emphasizes the complex interplay of multiple health issues within a single individual, rather than treating each condition in isolation.

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** multimorbidity, polypharmacy (related concept, often co-occurs with comorbidity)
