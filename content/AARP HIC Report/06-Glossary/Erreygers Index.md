**Term:**
`Erreygers Index`

**Definition:**
The Erreygers Index is a statistical measure used in health economics and public health to quantify the degree of socioeconomic inequality in a health variable. It is a corrected version of the standard Concentration Index, specifically designed to address its limitations when applied to bounded variables, such as binary health outcomes (e.g., alive/dead, sick/healthy) or ordinal scales (e.g., self-assessed health). The index measures the extent to which a health variable is concentrated among higher or lower-ranking individuals in a socioeconomic distribution, providing a value that ranges from -1 to +1.

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** Erreygers' corrected concentration index, Normalized concentration index
