**Term:**
`weathering`

**Definition:**
In a socioeconomic and public health context, particularly when applied to the experiences of Black, Indigenous, and [[People of Color]] (BIPOC), "weathering" refers to the cumulative physiological and psychological toll that chronic exposure to systemic racism, discrimination, and socioeconomic adversity takes on individuals over their lifespan. This concept posits that the constant stress of navigating oppressive systems leads to accelerated health deterioration and premature aging, manifesting as higher rates of chronic diseases and poorer health outcomes at younger ages.

**Related Terms:**
-   **Synonyms:** (Socioeconomic) cumulative stress, health disparities, accelerated aging, chronic adversity, systemic burden.
