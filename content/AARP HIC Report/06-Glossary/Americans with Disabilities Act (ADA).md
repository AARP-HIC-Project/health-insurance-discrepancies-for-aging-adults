**Term:**
`Americans with Disabilities Act`

**Definition:**
The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) is a comprehensive civil rights law enacted in the United States in 1990 that prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in all areas of public life, including employment, public services, public accommodations, transportation, and telecommunications. Its purpose is to ensure that people with disabilities have the same rights and opportunities as everyone else. The ADA mandates reasonable accommodations for employees with disabilities and accessibility requirements for public places.
