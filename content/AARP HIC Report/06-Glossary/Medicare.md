**Term:**  
`Medicare`

**Definition:** 
Medicare is a federal health insurance program in the United States that primarily provides health coverage for individuals aged 65 or older, certain younger people with disabilities, and people with End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) or Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS). It is funded by a combination of payroll taxes, beneficiary premiums, and general federal revenues, aiming to ensure access to essential medical services for eligible populations.

**Related Terms:** Medicaid, Medigap, Medicare Advantage (Part C), Medicare Part D, Social Security, Affordable Care Act (ACA), TRICARE.
