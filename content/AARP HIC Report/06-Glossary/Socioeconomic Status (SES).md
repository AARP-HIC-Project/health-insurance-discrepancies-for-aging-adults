**Term:**  
`Socioeconomic Status (SES)`

**Definition:** 
Socioeconomic status is a composite measure of an individual's or family's economic and social standing in relation to others, based on a combination of factors. The most common components used to determine SES are income, level of education, and occupation. It provides a comprehensive view of a person's position within a social hierarchy and is a key predictor of a wide range of life outcomes, including health, well-being, and access to resources.

**Related Terms:**  
- **Synonyms:** Socioeconomic position, social standing, social class (Note: "social class" is often used more in a theoretical context related to power and production, while SES is an empirical measure).
