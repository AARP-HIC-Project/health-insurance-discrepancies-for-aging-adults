**Term:**
`Affordable Care Act`

**Definition:**
The Affordable Care Act (ACA), also known as Obamacare, is a comprehensive federal statute enacted in the United States in 2010. Its primary goals are to expand health insurance coverage, control healthcare costs, and improve the quality of healthcare for Americans. The ACA introduced various reforms, including the establishment of health insurance marketplaces, subsidies to help individuals afford coverage, expansion of Medicaid eligibility, and new regulations for insurance companies, such as prohibiting denial of coverage due to pre-existing conditions.

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** Obamacare
