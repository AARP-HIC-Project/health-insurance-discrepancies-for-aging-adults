**Term:**
`Federal Poverty Level (FPL)`

**Definition:**
The Federal Poverty Level (FPL) is a set of income thresholds used by the U.S. government to determine who is considered poor and eligible for various federal assistance programs. These thresholds are updated annually by the Department of Health and Human Services and vary based on family size and composition. The FPL serves as a national benchmark for assessing economic need and allocating resources.

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** Poverty line, poverty threshold, income poverty.
