**Term:**
`Medicaid`

**Definition:**
Medicaid is a joint federal and state government program in the United States that provides health coverage to millions of Americans, including eligible low-income adults, children, pregnant women, elderly adults, and people with disabilities. It serves as a crucial safety net, ensuring access to medical care for individuals and families who might otherwise be unable to afford it. The program's design allows states significant flexibility in determining eligibility criteria, benefits, and payment rates, leading to variations in coverage across different states.

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** Medical assistance, public health insurance, government healthcare
