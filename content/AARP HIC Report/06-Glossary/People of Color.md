**Term:**
`People of Color`

**Definition:**
A collective term primarily used in the United States and other Western contexts to refer to non-white racial and ethnic groups. It emphasizes shared experiences of systemic racism, discrimination, and marginalization, distinguishing these groups from the dominant white population. The term is often preferred over "minority" as it avoids implying numerical inferiority and highlights shared identity, political solidarity, and agency among diverse non-white communities.

**Related Terms:**
-   **Synonyms:** BIPOC (Black, Indigenous, and People of Color), racialized groups, non-white individuals. (Note: "Minority" is often used in similar contexts but carries different connotations and is sometimes avoided.)
