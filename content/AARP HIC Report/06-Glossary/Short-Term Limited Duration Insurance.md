**Term:**
`Short-Term Limited Duration Insurance`

**Definition:**
Short-Term Limited Duration Insurance (STLDI), often referred to as short-term health insurance, is a type of health insurance coverage designed to provide temporary medical benefits for a limited period. Unlike plans compliant with the Affordable Care Act (ACA), STLDI plans are not required to cover essential health benefits, may deny coverage or charge higher premiums based on an applicant's health status or pre-existing conditions, and typically have annual and lifetime limits on benefits. They are intended to bridge gaps in coverage, such as during job transitions or waiting periods for other insurance.

**Related Terms:**
-   **Synonyms:** Short-term health insurance, temporary health insurance, limited benefit plans
