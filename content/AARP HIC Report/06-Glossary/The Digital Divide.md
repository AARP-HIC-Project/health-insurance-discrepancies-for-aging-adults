**Term:**
`The digital divide`

**Definition:**
The digital divide refers to the socioeconomic and demographic disparity between those who have ready access to information and communication technologies (ICTs), such as the internet, computers, and mobile devices, and those who do not. This gap encompasses not only physical access to technology but also the availability of affordable services, the possession of necessary [[Digital Literacy]] skills, access to relevant content, and the ability to effectively utilize these technologies for personal, educational, economic, and social advancement.

**Related Terms:**
-   **Synonyms:** Digital exclusion, information poverty, technology gap, access gap, digital inequality.
