**Term:**  
`culturally competent care`

**Definition:** 
Culturally competent care refers to the ability of healthcare providers and systems to deliver services that are respectful of and responsive to the health beliefs, practices, and cultural and linguistic needs of diverse patients. It involves understanding and appreciating the cultural context of individuals and families, recognizing and addressing biases, and adapting care to meet specific cultural requirements to improve health outcomes and reduce health disparities-- or centering a healthcare plan around [[Cultural Competency|cultural competency]]. This approach moves beyond mere awareness to active engagement and adaptation. 

**Related Terms:**  
- **Synonyms:** Cultural sensitivity, cultural humility, cultural responsiveness, inclusive care, patient-centered care (with cultural emphasis), [[Cultural Competency]]
