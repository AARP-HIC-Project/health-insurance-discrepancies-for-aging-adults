**Term:**
`Modified Adjusted Gross Income`

**Definition:**
Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI) is a specific calculation of an individual's or household's income used by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) and other government agencies to determine eligibility for various tax credits, deductions, and government programs. It typically begins with Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) and then adds back certain income items that were previously excluded or deducted, such as tax-exempt interest, non-taxable Social Security benefits, and certain foreign earned income exclusions. The precise components added back to AGI to arrive at MAGI can vary depending on the specific program for which eligibility is being determined.
