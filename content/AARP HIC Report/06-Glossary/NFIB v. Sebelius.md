**Term:**
`NFIB v. Sebelius`

**Definition:**
*NFIB v. Sebelius* (2012) was a landmark United States Supreme Court case that largely upheld the constitutionality of the Affordable Care Act (ACA), also known as Obamacare. The Court ruled that the ACA's individual mandate, which required most Americans to obtain health insurance or pay a penalty, was a permissible exercise of Congress's power to tax, rather than its power to regulate interstate commerce. However, the Court also limited the ACA's expansion of Medicaid, ruling that states could not be coerced into expanding their programs by threatening to withhold existing Medicaid funding.
