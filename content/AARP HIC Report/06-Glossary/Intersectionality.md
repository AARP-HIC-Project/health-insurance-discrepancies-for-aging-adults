**Term:**
`Intersectionality`

**Definition:**
Intersectionality is a theoretical framework that posits that social categorizations such as race, class, gender, sexuality, and disability, among others, do not exist independently but are interconnected and create overlapping and interdependent systems of discrimination or disadvantage. It highlights how individuals can experience multiple forms of oppression or privilege simultaneously, based on the unique combination of their social identities, leading to distinct experiences that cannot be understood by examining each identity axis in isolation (coined by legal scholar Kimberlé Crenshaw in 1989).

**Related Terms:**
-   **Synonyms:** Interlocking oppressions, multidimensional oppression, compound discrimination
