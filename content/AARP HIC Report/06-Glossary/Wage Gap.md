**Term:**
`wage gap`

**Definition:**
The wage gap refers to the average difference in earnings between two groups of people, typically categorized by gender, race, ethnicity, or other demographic factors. It is often expressed as a percentage or a dollar amount. The term can refer to an "unadjusted" or "raw" gap, which is the simple difference in median or average earnings, or an "adjusted" or "controlled" gap, which attempts to account for factors such as occupation, education, experience, and hours worked.

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** Pay disparity, earnings gap, income inequality (broader term), pay equity gap.
- **Antonyms:** Pay equity, equal pay.
