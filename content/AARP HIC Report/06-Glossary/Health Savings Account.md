**Term:**
`Health Savings Accounts`

**Definition:**
A Health Savings Account (HSA) is a tax-advantaged savings account established in the United States that can be used for qualified medical expenses. It is exclusively available to individuals who are enrolled in a High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) and offers a triple tax advantage: contributions are tax-deductible, earnings grow tax-free, and withdrawals for qualified medical expenses are tax-free. HSAs are portable, meaning they belong to the individual even if they change employers or health plans, and unlike some other healthcare savings vehicles, the funds roll over year to year.

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** None directly, but related financial tools for healthcare expenses include: Flexible Spending Account (FSA), Medical Savings Account (MSA - a precursor to the HSA).
