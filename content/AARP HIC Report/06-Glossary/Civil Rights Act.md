**Term:**  
`Civil Rights Act`

**Definition:** 
The Civil Rights Act primarily refers to the landmark federal legislation enacted in the United States in 1964, which outlaws discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin. It prohibits unequal application of voter registration requirements, racial segregation in schools, employment, and public accommodations. While the 1964 Act is the most comprehensive, the term can also refer to other significant U.S. federal statutes aimed at protecting civil rights, such as the Civil Rights Act of 1866 or the Civil Rights Act of 1968 (Fair Housing Act).

**Related Legislation:** Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA), [[Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)]] (ADA), Fair Housing Act (Civil Rights Act of 1968), Voting Rights Act of 1965
