**Term:**
`employer-sponsored health insurance (ESI)`

**Definition:**
Employer-sponsored health insurance (ESI) is a type of health coverage provided by an employer to its employees and, often, their dependents. It typically involves the employer contributing a portion of the premium costs, with employees paying the remainder through payroll deductions. ESI is a significant component of the healthcare system in many countries, particularly the United States, serving as a primary source of health coverage for working individuals and their families.

**Related Terms:**
-   **Synonyms:** Group health insurance, employer-provided health insurance, workplace health plan
