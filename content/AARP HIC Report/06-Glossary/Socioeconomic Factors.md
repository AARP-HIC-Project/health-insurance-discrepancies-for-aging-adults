**Term:**
`Socioeconomic Factors`

**Definition:**
Socioeconomic factors are the social and economic elements that collectively influence an individual's or group's position within society. They encompass a range of measurable attributes such as income, education level, occupation, wealth, and social class, which often correlate with health outcomes, life opportunities, and access to resources. These factors highlight the interconnectedness of social standing and economic conditions in shaping life experiences.

- **Synonyms:** [[Social Determinants of Health]], [[Socioeconomic Status (SES)]], social class, economic status, income level, wealth.
