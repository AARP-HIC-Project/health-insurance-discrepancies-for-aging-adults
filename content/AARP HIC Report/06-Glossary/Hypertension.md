**Term:**  
`hypertension`

**Definition:** 
Hypertension, commonly known as high blood pressure, is a chronic medical condition characterized by the persistently elevated force of blood against the walls of the arteries. This sustained elevation requires the heart to work harder to pump blood throughout the body, which, over time, can lead to serious health complications such as heart disease, stroke, kidney failure, and vision impairment. It is typically diagnosed when blood pressure readings consistently exceed established thresholds, such as 130/80 mmHg (systolic/diastolic) according to current medical guidelines.

**Related Terms:**  
- **Synonyms:** high blood pressure, elevated blood pressure
