# Recommendations for AARP

## Advocate for Comprehensive and Accessible Health Coverage Tailored to Aging Needs

Should political forces lead to significant cuts in Medicare and Medicaid, social service and advocacy organizations like AARP would face a severe dual challenge. First, there would be an immediate and substantial increase in demand for direct assistance and resources from aging adults who lose coverage or benefits. Second, the political environment itself would become significantly more challenging for advocacy efforts.

AARP should vigorously advocate against cuts to Medicare and Medicaid, championing enhanced ACA Marketplace subsidies. AARP should advocate for policies that not only expand coverage but also address structural barriers to care, such as low Medicaid reimbursement rates and lack of accessible facilities. This advocacy is crucial for aging adults, particularly as they transition from employer-sponsored insurance (ESI) to Medicare, a period where coverage gaps are observed to widen. 

Furthermore, AARP should champion initiatives to ensure that all insurance plans include essential benefits vital for older adults, such as dental, vision, and robust mental health services. Reducing enrollment barriers and standardizing eligibility requirements across states will not only directly benefit aging populations by ensuring consistent access to care but also contribute to a more equitable and robust national health insurance landscape.

## Address Systemic Barriers to Insurance Affordability and Healthcare Equity for Older Adults Specifically

AARP should promote systemic changes that address the fundamental social determinants of health impacting aging adults beyond direct insurance coverage. This includes advocating for policies that foster employment equity, ensure affordable housing, and strengthen digital infrastructure, all of which are critical for the well-being and healthcare access of older populations. 

Expand culturally competent outreach and enrollment assistance programs. Develop resources and workshops to enhance digital literacy for older adults, facilitating telehealth use. This will facilitate their ability to access and effectively utilize telehealth services and other emerging digital health technologies, ensuring they are not left behind by technological advancements.

It is imperative to address structural impediments to care, such as low Medicaid reimbursement rates and the lack of accessible facilities, which disproportionately affect aging individuals, particularly those with disabilities or from minority groups. 

Offer financial literacy programs to help members navigate healthcare costs. Foster collaborations with local community organizations and rural health centers to expand access to care.

Targeted interventions for aging adults are necessary because are recommended to cater to the unique needs of diverse aging populations, especially given the identified gap in post-pandemic research concerning disability and health insurance coverage, thereby fostering a more inclusive and responsive healthcare system for all.

## Enhance Health Literacy and Community-Based Support Systems for Aging Populations

AARP should develop and expand culturally competent outreach and enrollment assistance programs specifically designed for older adults. Creating educational resources and workshops to improve digital and financial literacy among aging individuals is essential, as these skills facilitate navigation of complex healthcare costs and effective utilization of telehealth services. 

Simplification of complex healthcare information and processes is paramount to enhance informed decision-making and program engagement among older adults. Additionally, fostering strong collaborations with local community organizations and rural health centers can significantly expand access to care for aging populations, acknowledging the profound role of community context in health outcomes and strengthening the overall healthcare support infrastructure.

Opportunities for enhanced community outreach and education could address some of the barriers related to awareness and system complexity, particularly for the complex enrollment process for Medicaid and marketplace insurance that is to come.

## Advance Evidence-Based Advocacy and Strategic Partnerships for Aging Health Policy

AARP should cultivate strategic alliances with other advocacy groups focused on disability rights, LGBTQ+ rights, racial justice, and rural health, as well as with healthcare providers, to collectively address the multifaceted challenges faced by aging adults. Leveraging data-driven insights is critical to inform policymakers and industry leaders, thereby promoting policy and product innovation that directly benefits older populations. 

Prioritized future research should include longitudinal studies on coverage transitions, assessments of the equitable impact of digital health interventions on older adults, and deeper intersectional analyses of compounded disadvantages within aging demographics. Furthermore, efforts should be made to address limitations in data collection by advocating for improved access to comprehensive reports, which are currently difficult to obtain, ensuring that policy decisions are grounded in robust evidence for the benefit of the entire health insurance landscape.
