**Term:**
`Coverage Gap`

**Definition:**
A coverage gap refers to a period during which an individual either lacks insurance coverage entirely, has significantly reduced coverage for specific services, or is responsible for a higher percentage of their healthcare costs. This can manifest in several ways, such as the historical "donut hole" in Medicare Part D where beneficiaries paid a larger share of prescription drug costs, the "ACA coverage gap" in states that did not expand Medicaid leaving low-income individuals without affordable options, or the 24-month waiting period for Medicare eligibility after qualifying for Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) during which individuals may be [[AARP HIC Report/06-Glossary/Uninsured]].

**Related Terms:**
- **Synonyms:** Donut hole (specific to Medicare Part D), period of uninsurance, lapse in coverage
