**Term:**
`COBRA`

**Definition:**
COBRA, an acronym for the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act, is a federal law enacted in 1985 that provides certain former employees, retirees, spouses, and dependent children the right to temporary continuation of health coverage at group rates. This coverage is available when coverage is lost due to specific "qualifying events," such as job loss (voluntary or involuntary, except for gross misconduct), reduction in hours, death of the employee, divorce or legal separation, or a dependent child ceasing to be a dependent under the plan rules. The primary purpose of COBRA is to bridge potential gaps in health insurance coverage during periods of transition.
